The Swaption Pricing Challenge

A swaption is a financial derivative granting the right to enter into an interest rate swap. Pricing depends on a 2D surface (14 tenors × 16 maturities = 224 features) that evolves daily. The task: predict the next 6 business days of this surface using ~494 historical rows.

Workload Distribution Strategy

Classical linear regression already captures ~95% of price variance (R² = 0.95). The quantum circuit learns only the residual ~5%, making the task tractable for NISQ hardware.

  • 💻
    Classical Baseline (LR) Captures 95% of structural pricing variance. RMSE = 0.0437
  • ⚛
    Quantum Residual (QRC) Fixed photonic circuit + enriched features corrects the remaining 5%. RMSE = 0.0392

Why QRC Beats Pure VQC

Our experiments reveal a striking gap: Residual QRC (RMSE 0.043) outperforms pure VQC (RMSE ~0.19) by 4.4×, despite training zero quantum parameters.

⚠️

Barren Plateaus

At 12+ modes, VQC gradients vanish exponentially. Training becomes futile. QRC sidesteps this by freezing the quantum circuit entirely.

🛠

Residual Architecture

Instead of predicting prices from scratch, the quantum circuit only corrects the ~5% residual error from a strong classical baseline (R² = 0.95).

🌱

Noise Resilience

Photon loss creates a different but equally useful feature space. The classical readout adapts to whatever the noisy circuit provides.
